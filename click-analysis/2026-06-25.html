The two tools measure different things

Beehiiv unique email clickers873
GA sessions tagged newsletter/email82
GA "direct" sessions (referrer stripped)722
Subscribe-page views (mostly Meta ads)578

*"Direct" is a catch-all GA uses when it can't read a source: typed URLs, bookmarks, privacy browsers, ad clicks that lost their tag — and email clicks, because most email apps strip the referrer. It is not purely newsletter traffic, so I won't claim a number for how much of the 722 is email. The "newsletter/email" line (82) only counts visits where Beehiiv's UTM tag actually survived. The proof email is hiding in "direct": Colbert's page drew 460 GA users — almost identical to Beehiiv's 458 unique email clickers — yet GA filed almost none of them under "newsletter." Takeaway: the 82 badly understates email's real influence; use Beehiiv for email engagement and GA for spotting non-email demand.

What clicks + traffic tell you together

  • An ICYMI item beat the lead 4-to-1. Colbert's Hometown Table — filed under ICYMI — pulled 528 clicks vs. the Grip, Rip and Dip lead's 126, and ~31% of all clicks in the issue. Per the 13% playbook, that's a loud lead-selection signal: a celebrity-anchored restaurant story is Tier-1 curiosity fuel and probably should have led. Worth a look at how the lead gets chosen.
  • Two Blokes had legs beyond the inbox. Only 79 email clicks but 192 web views — nearly 60% of its traffic came from Meta ads, Nextdoor, and social, not the newsletter. When a story keeps drawing after send, that's a candidate to push harder on paid + social.
  • Your biggest web magnet wasn't a story — it was /subscribe (578 views), almost entirely from Meta ads (307 sessions, 566 views). Paid acquisition is pulling its weight; the newsletter click report would never have shown you this.
  • Nextdoor is a sleeper. 145 sessions / 210 views with zero effort — worth a deliberate test of posting there weekly.
  • Events drew ~17% of clicks — real, but a supporting act. 296 clicks across 16 venues, second only to ICYMI (47%). But it's broad and shallow: the top single event (Princess Bride / Cary Elwes, 46) was under 3% on its own. Readers graze the section rather than converging, and marquee named events out-pull generic venue calendars 2-3x. Judgment: lead the events block with one or two big named draws and trim or roll the long tail into the calendar link — and treat the marquee slot, not the long list, as the sponsorship lever.
  • Mind the attribution gap. Beehiiv counted 873 email clickers; GA tagged only 82 as "newsletter" because email apps strip the referrer. I won't claim how much of GA's 722 "direct" is email — it's a mixed bucket — but Colbert's near-identical Beehiiv-vs-GA user counts prove email is heavily under-credited. Use Beehiiv for email engagement, GA for discovering non-email demand.
  • Two universes, now joined. External links (venues, marketchs, Dinner Club) live on other domains and never hit this GA property — so Beehiiv is the only window into those. Together the two tools finally give you the full picture of each issue.
