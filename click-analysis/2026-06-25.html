Reading these two systems together

GA "direct" is a mixed bucket — typed URLs, bookmarks, privacy browsers, lost ad tags, and email clicks whose referrer got stripped in transit. We can't put a number on how much of that 774 is email, and we won't pretend to.

But the under-crediting is visible. The lead-page comparison tells it: Colbert's drew 466 GA web users, almost dead-on the 461 unique email clickers Beehiiv counted for the same link — yet GA tagged only ~86 sessions all week as "newsletter." Those readers came from the email; GA just filed most of them under "direct."

Bottom line: trust Beehiiv for email engagement, GA for non-email demand (Meta ads sent 410 sessions and 774 subscribe-page views, Nextdoor another 161). Don't stack the two — you'll double-count the same humans.

Takeaways

  • An ICYMI lapped the lead, again Colbert's Hometown Table pulled 532 clicks — 4x the actual lead (Grip Rip & Dip, 128). Stephen Colbert's name is a Tier-1 curiosity hook. Per the 13% playbook, that should have led
  • Curiosity + celebrity beats "coming soon" The lead was a new-opening teaser; the winner was a name-drop verdict. Next time a Colbert-caliber hook is in the stack, put it up top and let the openings ride ICYMI
  • Events drew broad and shallow 16.1% of clicks spread across 14 venue links, none cracking 46. Reliable engagement filler, not a traffic driver — good for sponsor "we send real foot traffic" proof, weak as a headline
  • marketchs.com had a monster week 101 clicks, the #3 link overall and your top TFG asset by a mile. Whatever placement it got, repeat it
  • Two Blokes over-indexed on web 194 web views vs 79 email clicks — the widest email-to-web gap in the issue. Brewery-crossover content travels beyond the inbox; a push-on-paid candidate
  • Healthy top line 71.1% open, 10.3% verified CTR, 0.20% unsubscribe. Engagement is holding as the list grows past 11.4K
