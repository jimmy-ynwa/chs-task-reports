Read This Before Trusting the Numbers

Why email is under-credited in GA

GA's "direct" bucket is a junk drawer: typed URLs, bookmarks, privacy browsers, ad tags that fell off, and email clicks whose referrer got stripped in transit. We can't say what slice of those 1,262 direct sessions is email, and we won't guess. But the fingerprint is clear: the lead story pulled 458 web users in GA against 408 unique email clickers in Beehiiv, while GA tagged fewer than 100 sessions as "newsletter." Those readers came from the email; GA just lost the label. Bottom line: trust Beehiiv for email engagement, trust GA for non-email demand (search, Nextdoor, ads), and remember external links (venues, City Paper) never touch this property at all.

Takeaways

  • The lead earned its slot. Food Network's North Charleston barbecue take grabbed 35% of every click and roughly 4x the #2 link. A verdict-style lead, exactly what the 13% playbook wants up top.
  • No ICYMI beat the lead. Best ICYMI was the Seafood Shop bar at 75 clicks, about 15% of the lead. Lead selection was correct, no buried headliner this week.
  • Cheap eats has legs. The City Paper "20 affordable places" roundup drew 130 clicks, second only to the lead and ahead of every one of our own ICYMI stories. That's an audience appetite we're handing to a competitor. Own it as CHS content next time.
  • Events ran broad and shallow. Twelve venue and Fourth-of-July links split 181 clicks with no single magnet above Hotel Bennett's 36. Holiday-week interest was real but scattered.
  • Web demand outran email credit. The lead drew more GA users than we had unique email clickers, yet GA tagged almost none as newsletter. Don't let the dashboard talk you out of the email's reach.
  • Growth is paid and referral, not organic. Nextdoor (547) and Meta ads (593) each drove more sessions than the entire newsletter, nearly all into /subscribe. The list is being fed by spend and Nextdoor, not search.
