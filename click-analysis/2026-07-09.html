The Nextdoor story

Nextdoor sent 442 organic sessions this week — nearly matching direct, and outpacing all paid Meta traffic combined. Charleston is sharing the Costco piece on its own. Free reach worth watching.

Reading the Attribution

GA "direct" (775 sessions) is a mixed bucket — typed URLs, bookmarks, privacy browsers, lost ad tags, and email clicks whose referrer got stripped. We can't put a clean number on how much of it is email. But the under-crediting is visible: the lead story drew 185 GA web users while Beehiiv logged ~160 unique email clicks on it, yet GA tags only 46 sessions as "newsletter." Bottom line — trust Beehiiv for email engagement, GA for non-email demand. External links (venues, the Farrell Group site) never touch this GA property at all.

Takeaways

  • An ICYMI beat the lead — Costco's "third store is finally real" (209) edged the Ladybird lead feature (199). Ten clicks, but a real signal: the concrete-development verdict out-pulled the human-interest profile.
  • The lead was a Tier 3 hook — "She bet on a bookstore" is a profile framing. Verified CTOR landed at 8.93%, right on the 8.3% baseline and far from the 13% ceiling. Next time a Costco-style verdict story is in the stack, lead with it.
  • Costco is the push-on-paid candidate — 650 web views and 398 users vs 205 email clicks. It over-indexed hard on non-email demand, most of it Nextdoor. Worth a boosted post.
  • Nextdoor is doing free work — 442 organic sessions, more than all Meta ads combined. Charleston is spreading the Costco story without us paying for it.
  • Events stayed broad and shallow — 15.2% of clicks spread across 15 venues, none cracking the top 5. Jeremiah Farm led at 50. Reliable filler, not a driver.
  • Community plays held — the lead magnet (71), Dinner Club (63), and the YouTube clip (84) all outclicked four of the five ICYMI stories. The owned surfaces are pulling their weight.
