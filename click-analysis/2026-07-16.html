Attribution caveat

GA4 wasn't reachable this run, so "site traffic" here comes from Beehiiv's own website analytics instead of the usual GA4 property — directional, not the exact cut Jimmy normally gets, and flag this to reconnect GA4 before next week's run.

Even with GA4, "direct" is always a mixed bucket: typed URLs, bookmarks, privacy browsers, lost ad tags, and email clicks whose referrer got stripped. Only 39 sessions this week were explicitly tagged as coming from a mail app, but Beehiiv logged 1,539 total email clicks for this single issue alone — a strong sign email traffic is landing in "direct" uncredited, not that email underperformed. Nextdoor and Meta Ads are legitimate non-email demand and should be trusted as such. External links (Eventbrite, venue sites, marketchs.com) never show up in on-site analytics at all, so this view only ever tells half the story for event clicks.

Takeaways

  • Bolchoz's Second Act should've probably led. It pulled 526 clicks as an ICYMI item, 34% of the whole issue's clicks and 24x the lead story's own self-link traffic. That's about as loud a lead-selection signal as this playbook gets.
  • Dashi's low click count isn't a red flag. The full story ran in-email, so its only clicks were view-online, share, and comment links (22 total). Nothing to fix here, just a different read pattern than click-through ICYMI stories.
  • Events drew broad, shallow interest. 268 clicks (17% of the issue) spread across 15-plus links, no single event breaking away from the pack. Fine for community goodwill, not a traffic engine.
  • El Molino and Bolchoz lean hardest on email. Both sit near 0.47-0.49 email-clicks-to-site-views, meaning email is doing real work getting readers there. Other stories pull more of their traffic from elsewhere (Nextdoor, Meta Ads).
  • Nextdoor and Meta Ads are now bigger non-email demand sources than Google for the site overall (327 and 180 sessions vs. 59). Diversification off email is paying off.
  • Reconnect GA4 before next week. This run substituted Beehiiv's own site analytics since the GA4 connector wasn't reachable — still useful, but not the exact numbers you're used to.
